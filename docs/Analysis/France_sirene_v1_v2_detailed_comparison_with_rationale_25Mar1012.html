Why the 16.8M → ~1.3K drop? We are filtering on company name only. SIRENE has only 3 searchable fields per company: registered name, trade name, and acronym. Out of 16.8M entities, exactly 1,326 contain the words "drone", "UAV", "RPAS", or "telepilot" in these name fields. The other 16,850,344 entities are simply not drone-related by name.

What's excluded in this drop (16,850,344 entities):
- 7.7M sole proprietors (auto-entrepreneurs, freelancers — vast majority not in drones)
- 2.5M SCI real estate holding companies
- 1.5M non-profit associations (sports clubs, charities)
- ~5.0M structured companies in every other industry (restaurants, shops, tech firms, factories...)

What we CANNOT do to reduce this drop:
No company descriptions SIRENE has no free-text "objet social" (corporate purpose) field. Only a NAF activity code (e.g., "71.12B") and the company name. Descriptions exist only in the RCS (Registre du Commerce) via Infogreffe — paid per document, no bulk access.
No website / email / LinkedIn SIRENE contains zero digital contact data. No way to crawl company websites from the registry alone.
No keyword in activity description NAF codes are broad categories (e.g., "Private security activities") — they don't mention "drone" even if the company deploys drones.
Why this matters: Companies like Groupe Protect (registered as "GROUPE PROTECTOR", NAF 80.10Z — security) and Altametris (registered as "ALTAMETRIS", NAF 63.11Z — data processing) are real drone operators but have zero drone-related text in their SIRENE record. They cannot be found by any name-based waterfall. This is a fundamental ceiling of the SIRENE-only approach — catching these "invisible operators" requires external signals (Google Search Crawler, LinkedIn, DJI Partner Scanner).

Why remove 504 photo/video/cleaning drone companies?
We verified via web search that these are legitimate drone operators — but in a different market segment from DJI Dock:
- 74.20Z photography (269): aerial photographers using DJI Mavic/Phantom for weddings, real estate, events. Manned flights, not autonomous Dock.
- 59.11B video (69): aerial videographers using DJI Inspire. Same — manned creative flights.
- 81.22Z cleaning (77): spray drone operators using Drone Volt Hercules / Lucid Bots Sherpa for facade washing. DJI Dock has no spray capability.

v2 keeps these in the Supabase table — tagged as "non_dock_drone" — for future market intelligence. They're just excluded from the Dock-focused scoring and BD outreach list.
Stage 4 — Scoring model

v1: ad-hoc scoring

0–48 scale

Hard cutoffs on age + employees

Young (<1yr) excluded. Employee ≥5 for top 50. ETI/GE scored higher.

v2: normalised /100

50–95 scale

No hard cutoffs, 5 weighted signals

Age: not scored (industry too young)
Size flipped: PME/micro=15, ETI/GE=5
Why flip the size scoring? Drone service providers are typically small firms (3-20 employees) funded by enterprise clients for inspection/surveillance projects. A PME with 10 employees deploying DJI Dock is a hotter lead than an ETI with 500 employees that merely has a small drone division. Why not score age? The DJI Dock market started growing in 2022-2023. A 6-month-old company deploying Dock 3 is as valuable as a 10-year-old firm. Penalising youth would exclude exactly the early adopters we want.

Known ceiling of SIRENE-only approach: This waterfall finds companies with "drone" in their name — ~13-20% of the total French drone market (~5,000-8,000 operators per DGAC estimates). The remaining 80% don't have "drone" in their registered name. SIRENE provides no company description, no website, no contact data, and no keyword-searchable activity field — only the company name + a broad NAF code. Companies like Groupe Protect and Altametris are invisible to any registry-based filter and require external signal sources (Google Search Crawler, LinkedIn company posts, DJI Partner Scanner) to discover. These form Track B of the discovery pipeline, planned as a future phase.
